# Growth strategy

Source: `frontend/src/utils/businessPlanContent.tsx` (the full pitch/business plan content — this file is the source of truth, this doc is a summary pointer to it, not a replacement).

## Pilot approach: eMadadeni first

The Mall is in the testing and pre-launch stage, focused on onboarding early vendors within a single township — **eMadadeni** — to build vendor density and a functional ecosystem before expanding elsewhere. The reasoning (per the business plan): prove the model in a smaller, controlled environment rather than spreading thin across multiple areas at once.

**Short-term goal (3–5 months from the plan's writing):** launch publicly within the pilot area, refine features based on real user feedback, and measure adoption/engagement before deciding how and where to expand next.

## Structure and stage

Sole proprietorship, founded and built by Banele Hlela (lead developer and founder). Currently prototype/MVP stage. Development follows Lean Startup methodology — incremental, feedback-driven iteration rather than a fixed upfront spec.

## What's being sought

Per the business plan, The Mall is actively seeking, alongside the eMadadeni launch:

- **Partnerships** — collaboration with organizations that can help reach or support township vendors.
- **Technical contributors** — the plan explicitly names needing developer collaboration beyond the current single-developer setup.
- **Accelerator/incubator programs** — for both product quality (mentorship) and business capacity (funding, structure) support.

## Why township-first, not broad e-commerce

The plan's competitive positioning: general platforms (Shopify-style tools, etc.) assume a level of capital and technical readiness most small/township businesses don't have, and don't offer the integrated social + marketplace + community dynamics The Mall is building. The township-first approach is a deliberate wedge into an underserved market rather than a resource constraint alone — see `businessPlanContent.tsx` for the full competitive analysis.
